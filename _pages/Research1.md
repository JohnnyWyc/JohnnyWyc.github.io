    <h2 style="margin-top:0;">Distributionally Robust Invariant Learning for OOD Generalization</h2>
    <p style="margin:0.2em 0 0.8em 0;">
      Related: <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.13539">arXiv</a>
    </p>

    <ul style="margin-top:0;">
      <li><strong>Problem.</strong> Invariant learning methods address correlation shift but remain vulnerable to diversity shift and support mismatch under severe OOD settings.</li>
      <li><strong>Method.</strong> Unified invariant risk minimization with sample-level Distributionally Robust Optimization (DRO) by introducing a learnable adversarial reweighting mechanism.</li>
      <li><strong>Contribution.</strong> Formulated a primal–dual–adversarial training objective that improves worst-case generalization across both classification and regression benchmarks.</li>
    </ul>

    <h2 style="margin-top:0;">Learning Attention Behaviors with Decision Trees</h2>
    <p style="margin:0.2em 0 0.8em 0;">
      Related: <a href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/2110.03879">arXiv</a>
    </p>

    <ul style="margin-top:0;">
      <li><strong>Problem.</strong> Attention mechanisms in end-to-end speech recognition are difficult to interpret and analyze temporally.</li>
      <li><strong>Method.</strong> Modeled attention dynamics using decision trees constructed from attention state transitions.</li>
      <li><strong>Contribution.</strong> Revealed strong temporal dependence in attention evolution and demonstrated that attention states are highly predictable (&gt;80% accuracy).</li>
    </ul>
